Italian Weddings

Engagement

Engagement for italian people is not so different to othets culturs. A man proporse to a woman directly giving her a ring with a diamond which is a symbol of the enternity of love. Engagement rings is a tradition that date back to medieval ages and is now one or the most common traditions all over the world.

Engagement Party

The couple organizes a meeting and invite both of their families to allow them to know each others.

Hen and Bachelorette Parties

A hen or bachelorette party in Italy is a dinner with the friends and the couple. The interesting is that even the ones who are not invited to the wedding. Is a night out makes one or two weekends before the wedding.

Superstitions Regarding Weddings

One superstition that have Italian people is that the bride spends the night before of her wedding at her parent’s house. Sometimes she wear green clothing because it is that brings a good fortune. The bride can´t use any gold on her wedding, apart of her ring because is considered like bad luck. The bride has to wear something old, something new, something borrowed , something blue, and something she has received as a gift.

Getting to the Ceremony

As is tradition in others cities, the groom must not see the Bride before the wedding ceremony. He wait with their witness in the altar while the bride step in accompanied by her father. The father kiss her and shakes the Groom´s hand and walks where is the Bride´s Mother. This is more common with Catholic weddings, while for a civil wedding Bride and Groom arrive together at the Wedding Hall accompanied by family and friends. In some regions Bride and Groom walk together to the Church, in other regions, the Groom brings the bouquet of flowers to the wedding or even carries a piece of iron in his pocket.

Italian Weddings Rings

The Italian rings exchange is the most moving moment of the marriage ceremony. The rings in Italy weddings are usually put on the left hand. This tradition dates back to the Roman times, when the left ring finger was believed to be connect directly to the heart by a special vein called ‘vena amoris’. The vein of love.

After the Ceremony

Rice is thrown at the couple coming out of the Church or wedding hall to symbolize a shower of fertility. All guests approach and congratulate the newlyweds outside the wedding venue. In some regions in Italy Bride and Groom have to cut a log in two with a double handle saw after the wedding. This symbolizes the couple's partnership in marriage. Another nice tradition is tying tying a ribbon in front of the church.

Wedding Reception

Many funny moments are organized at an Italian Wedding despite the main activity is still eating a lot of delicious and genuine food accompanied by the best wines, limoncello, and liquors. At some weddings, particularly in Northern Italy, the best man cuts the groom's tie into little pieces. The pieces are then put onto a trayand sold to the guests. Bride and Groom go from table to table, greeting and chatting with every guest, so everyone feels welcomed.

At some point friends of the couple sneak away to play tricks in the new couple's house (they put itching powder in the bed, fishes in the bathtub, hide shoes...), others organize practical jokes for the couple.

It is customarily that the Bride throws her bouquet to the single ladies attending the banquet. Whoever catches the bouquet will be the next to get married! The wedding cake is white (for purity), tiered, and topped by figurines representing the bride and groom. sometimes the very close friends stay with the couple even after the reception, they go all together for a drink or even accompany them to their new house.

Bomboniere

At the end of the wedding day, just before guests leave, Bride and Groom give small gifts to their guests to say thank you. Italians call them bomboniere, made of a present, confetti (sugared almonds), and printed ribbons. Italian Bomboniere with confetti are a symbol of family life. The number of confetti is very important: it should be an odd number - preferably 5 or 7- each a good-luck number.
